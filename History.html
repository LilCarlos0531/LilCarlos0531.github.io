ABOUT THE HISTORY OF ROBOTICS

1941, Writer Isaac Asimov lays out three laws of robotics, 1) A robot may not injure a human being or, through inaction, allow a human being to come to harm. 2) A robot must obey the orders given to it by human beings except where such orders would conflict with the First Law. 3) A robot must protect its own existence as long as such protection does not conflict with the First or Second Law. 1961, The first mass-produced industrial robot goes to work at General Motors to help with car manufacturing. The heavy robot originally came from a mechanical arm design by inventor George Devol. 1969, Inventor Victor Scheinman introduces the “Stanford Arm,” considered to be the first electric robotic arm to be controlled by a computer. The robot could use its sensors to assemble a water pump for a Model A Ford vehicle. 1997, Nasa's Mars Pathfinder is the first ever robotic rover to land on the surface of Mars. 2002, iRobot's Roomba goes to the market, the robots uses sensors and computer processing to naviagte around.

2013, Boston Dynamics reveals Atlas, a six-foot-tall humanoid robot that can do gymnastics and parkour! 2015, Hitchbot, a hitchhiking robot, makes its way across Germany, the Netherlands, and Canada, before being vandalized and rendered inoperable :( . 2017,Waymo, begins testing its autonomous minivans in Arizona; marking a milestone for autonomous vehicles. 2021, Amazon releases its first homebot named Astro, which is powered by Alexa and can follow a user around their home or conduct home monitoring using a 3D map which is pretty cool if you ask me!

The Future of Robotics Robotic engineers are designing the next generation of robots to look, feel and act more human, to make it easier for us to warm up to a cold machine. I believe that in the future many jobs will be replaced with robots and we humans we have to adapt to working with a machine in our daily lives.
